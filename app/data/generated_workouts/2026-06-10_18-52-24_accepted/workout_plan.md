**Focus**: Upper Body Strength & Hypertrophy
**Duration**: Approximately 45 minutes

**1. Warm-up (5 minutes)**
*   **Light Cardio**: 2-3 minutes of marching in place, light jumping jacks (if comfortable for legs), or arm circles.
*   **Dynamic Stretches**:
    *   Arm Circles (forward & backward): 10-15 reps each direction
    *   Scapular Retractions/Protraction (shoulder squeezes): 10-12 reps
    *   Cat-Cow Stretch (on mat): 8-10 reps
    *   Bodyweight Squats (shallow, controlled, only if comfortable for sore legs): 5-8 reps, focusing on mobility.

**2. Main Workout (35 minutes)**
Perform the exercises in order. Aim for controlled movements and progressive overload where possible (more reps, slightly heavier dumbbells if form allows). Rest 60-90 seconds between sets.

*   **1. Dumbbell Floor Press**
    *   **Sets**: 3-4
    *   **Reps**: 8-12
    *   **Weight**: Start with 15 kg per hand. If form allows and it feels good, try to reach 10-12 reps.
    *   **Notes**: Lie on your back on the mat with knees bent and feet flat. Hold a dumbbell in each hand, elbows on the floor at shoulder level. Press the dumbbells straight up until your arms are fully extended, then slowly lower them back down until your triceps lightly touch the floor.

*   **2. Band-Assisted Pull-ups (on pull-up bar)**
    *   **Sets**: 3
    *   **Reps**: 6-10
    *   **Assistance**: Use a resistance band for assistance. Since band-assisted pull-ups were noted as harder to control than the gravitron in the past (workout 006), start with a band that offers sufficient assistance to get 6-7 good reps. You used 15kg assistance on gravitron for 8 reps last time (workout 010), so aim for a band that feels equivalent or slightly more assistance to maintain reps.
    *   **Notes**: Loop a resistance band over the pull-up bar and place one or both feet into the loop. Grip the bar with an overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulder-width. Pull yourself up until your chin clears the bar, focusing on squeezing your lats. Slowly lower yourself down.

*   **3. Dumbbell Overhead Triceps Extension**
    *   **Sets**: 3
    *   **Reps**: 10-12
    *   **Weight**: Choose a dumbbell you can control for 10-12 reps (e.g., 10-15 kg).
    *   **Notes**: Sit on a mat or stand. Hold one dumbbell with both hands, extending it overhead. Keeping your elbows close to your head, slowly lower the dumbbell behind your head by bending your elbows. Extend your arms to push the dumbbell back up.

*   **4. Dumbbell Bicep Curl**
    *   **Sets**: 3
    *   **Reps**: 10-12
    *   **Weight**: Use 10 kg per hand. Try to hit 12 reps if possible, as you hit 12 reps for your first set last time (workout 010).
    *   **Notes**: Stand or sit with a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing forward. Keeping your elbows tucked in, curl the dumbbells up towards your shoulders. Squeeze your biceps at the top, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down.

*   **5. Plank**
    *   **Sets**: 2
    *   **Duration**: 50-60 seconds
    *   **Notes**: Hold a strong, straight line from head to heels, engaging your core and glutes. Breathe deeply.

**3. Cool-down (5 minutes)**
*   **Gentle Stretching**:
    *   Triceps Stretch (one arm overhead, gently pull elbow): 30 seconds per arm
    *   Biceps Stretch (extend arm, palm up, gently pull fingers back): 30 seconds per arm
    *   Chest Stretch (hands clasped behind back or against a wall): 30-45 seconds
    *   Upper Back/Lat Stretch (reach overhead, gentle side bend): 30 seconds per side
*   **Deep Breathing**: Finish with a minute of slow, deep breaths to bring your heart rate down.
